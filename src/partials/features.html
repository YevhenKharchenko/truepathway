RULES & FEATURES

  • Continuous Connection

    A valid solution must create one unbroken line from origin to destination.
    No gaps.
    No dead ends.
    No side loops.

  • False Routes

    Some paths will look correct at first.
    They may connect halfway across the board.
    But hidden breaks or misaligned angles will invalidate them.
    Only one configuration completes the true pathway.

  • Tile Rotation

    Each tile rotates 90 degrees per interaction.
    Planning multiple moves ahead prevents unnecessary rotations.
    The fewer moves you make, the better your score will be.

  • Efficiency Scoring

    Players are rewarded for:
    Minimal rotations
    Clean alignment
    No redundant moves
    Fast logical recognition
    You can always find a better way to solve it if you try.

  • Things Get Trickier

    As levels advance, new mechanics appear:
    Locked tiles
    Multi-layer grids
    Directional one-way segments
    Limited rotation allowances
    Split start points merging into one path

Sure, the difficulty ramps up, but it's the good kind of challenging - not the kind that makes you want to chuck your phone across the room.
