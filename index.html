True Pathway: Line of Alignment

Not every route leads forward.

A grid of rotating tiles.
Multiple possible paths.
Only one alignment connects start to destination without interruption.

Your task is simple:
Rotate carefully. Think ahead. Commit deliberately.

The board may look complete.
But only one pathway is true.

Learn the Rules

A Puzzle of Direction

Each level presents a structured grid.
Tiles contain path segments that can be rotated.

At first glance, many routes appear valid.
But subtle misalignments create false connections.

The goal is not just connection - it is correct connection.

When you finally nail it, there's this amazing 'aha!' moment - kinda like solving a Rubik's cube, but maybe even better because you had to really think it through.

Core Gameplay

  • Define Your Path

    First, take a look at where you're starting and where you need to end up.

  • Rotate the Tiles

    Rotate tiles to connect segments.

  • Ensure Continuity

    Make sure your path doesn't have any breaks or gaps in it.

  • Avoid False Routes

    Avoid false routes that appear correct but break further ahead.

  • Optimize Your Moves

    Complete the pathway using the fewest rotations possible.

There are no timers.
Don't rush it - this game's all about thinking things through, not racing against the clock.

RULES & FEATURES

  • Continuous Connection

    A valid solution must create one unbroken line from origin to destination.
    No gaps.
    No dead ends.
    No side loops.

  • False Routes

    Some paths will look correct at first.
    They may connect halfway across the board.
    But hidden breaks or misaligned angles will invalidate them.
    Only one configuration completes the true pathway.

  • Tile Rotation

    Each tile rotates 90 degrees per interaction.
    Planning multiple moves ahead prevents unnecessary rotations.
    The fewer moves you make, the better your score will be.

  • Efficiency Scoring

    Players are rewarded for:
    Minimal rotations
    Clean alignment
    No redundant moves
    Fast logical recognition
    You can always find a better way to solve it if you try.

  • Things Get Trickier

    As levels advance, new mechanics appear:
    Locked tiles
    Multi-layer grids
    Directional one-way segments
    Limited rotation allowances
    Split start points merging into one path

Sure, the difficulty ramps up, but it's the good kind of challenging - not the kind that makes you want to chuck your phone across the room.

From Simple Lines to Layered Networks

  • Early levels focus on straightforward linear connections.

  • Later stages introduce: Larger grid sizes. Misleading symmetry

  • Rotational traps
    Multiple near-correct pathways
    Even when it gets more complex, the screen doesn't feel overwhelming.
    But it's still easy to follow what's happening.
